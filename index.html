// 01 — About

Geoscientist &
Researcher

I am a PhD researcher at the Indian Institute of Technology, Kanpur, specializing in seismotectonics, impact geology, and structural earth sciences. My academic journey spans IIT Kharagpur (M.Sc) and Durgapur Government College (B.Sc), culminating in a CPI of 9.83/10 at the doctoral level.

My research bridges the microscopic — examining microfracture densities and XRD signatures in impact rocks — to the macroscopic, reconstructing crustal deformation histories across the Himalayan arc and northeast India. I have led multiple field expeditions to the Lonar Impact Crater, one of the world's best-preserved meteorite impact structures.

Outside the lab, I am a painting enthusiast (Bharat Kala Kendra, 5th year, 1st Division) and an avid cricketer — disciplines that sharpen the patience and precision my science demands.
